Machine Learning Ops

MLOps (Machine Learning Operations) is the set of practices that unifies ML system development with IT operations. It applies DevOps principles — automation, monitoring, and continuous delivery — to the machine learning lifecycle.

Core components

  • Data pipelines — versioned, reproducible ingestion and transformation
  • Model registry — tracks trained artefacts, metadata, and lineage
  • CI/CD for ML — automated retraining, evaluation, and deployment gates
  • Monitoring — data drift, concept drift, latency, and error tracking in production

Relationship to related concepts

MLOps sits downstream of Retrieval-Augmented Generation infrastructure and upstream of cost concerns covered in Q: Cost comparison — GPT-4 vs Claude. Teams using Fine-tuning vs. Prompting workflows will find MLOps tooling most relevant when they commit to fine-tuning.

Answer
Based on the pages gathered — particularly Fine-tuning vs. Prompting, Retrieval-Augmented Generation, and Vector Embeddings — here are the key trade-offs: Fine-tuning works best when you need consistent tone, format, or domain vocabulary baked into the model's weights. The downside is that knowledge becomes stale the moment the training data is, and retraining cycles are expensive. Your MLOps page notes that this path requires a full model registry and CI/CD pipeline. RAG keeps knowledge fresh — you update the document store, not the model. Latency is higher (retrieval + generation), and quality depends heavily on your embedding model and chunking strategy. The Vector Embeddings page flags that dense retrieval can miss exact-match queries that BM25 handles trivially.
